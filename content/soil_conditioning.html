Soil Conditioning

Soil conditioning works by improving the biology and structure of the soil, rather than simply adding fertilizer.

Your tree's favorite probiotic

These carbon-based amendments feed beneficial soil microorganisms, which in turn help release nutrients that are already present but unavailable to the tree.

By increasing microbial activity, soil conditioners improve soil structure, reduce compaction, and enhance water and oxygen movement in the root zone.

For trees, this results in stronger root systems, improved nutrient uptake, and greater resilience to stress from drought, heat, and poor soil conditions.
